Toronto Islands


About

The Toronto Islands - also called the Island, or Toronto Island Park - is located in Lake Ontario, a 13-minute ferry ride from downtown Toronto. Boats to the Island leave from the Jack Layton Ferry Terminal at the foot of Bay St. and Queenâ€™s Quay. The Island is actually a group of 15 islands inter-connected by pathways and bridges. You can walk from one end of the Island to the other. The island is approximately 5 kms long from Ward's Island to Hanlan's Point.

Tickets (including return ticket)

Adults: $7.87
Seniors or Students: $5.16
Youth (4-13): $3.80

Directions

By TTC or GO transit
Take the subway or GO train to Union station. It's a ten minute walk south from the ferry docks at the foot of Bay Street. Or take the Streetcar one stop from Union Station to the Island Ferry Docks. The docks is also served by the Bay St. bus. Get off at the Queen's Quay stop.
